NOTE TO FILE — 2025 tax year
Adrian M. Holloway / Holloway household, Greenwich CT

Writing this down before my first meeting with the new accountant, because I want to get organized
and, honestly, I don't fully understand my own situation.

Background: most of what we live on comes from my grandfather's trust — the Ashworth Family
Settlement, set up in Jersey. I get $10,000 a month from it ($120,000 for the year). On paper the
trust is worth about $2.1 million, but I can't touch the principal until I turn 45, so it isn't
money I can actually get at. I also do a bit of independent consulting on the side (Brightwater
Partners) — not much, but it's why I call myself a consultant.

What's been keeping me up: my grandfather's lawyers used to "handle the trust paperwork," and
nobody ever really explained what that meant. Now that they're out of the picture I've realized the
trust is a foreign one, and I get the sense there's more to the U.S. side of all this than I've
been keeping on top of. I never set anything up with the IRS during the year, because, again, "it
was handled." I'd rather walk into the meeting having actually looked at my own paperwork than have
the accountant explain it to me cold.

And the part that's almost funny: on paper I'm a millionaire, but my debit card got declined at
brunch last month. We have about $3,000 in checking, roughly $22,000 spread across three credit
cards, the big mortgage, and the Range Rover lease. The money comes in and goes right back out.

I've pulled everything I could find into this folder: the trust forms, the trustee's annual
accounts, the investment manager's statements and tax voucher, the foreign-account paperwork, my
consulting records, the mortgage year-end statement, the bank statement and the full-year
transactions, and the three credit-card statements. There's also a half-finished spreadsheet I
started ("tax_tracker_DRAFT") — I'm not confident in it at all, so please don't lean on my numbers.
I just want to understand where we really stand for 2025.

— A.H.
